Wind Turbine Production Simulation
AnyLogicDigital TwinDES
A leading industrial client needed to justify a major crane investment for their wind turbine assembly line. Conventional capacity planning could not capture stochastic variability, equipment breakdowns, and shift interdependencies — the management required evidence, not assumptions. The objective was to evaluate the impact of a new crane on overall productivity and determine whether the existing material handling system constituted a bottleneck.
A discrete-event simulation (DES) model was developed in AnyLogic to represent the relevant production segment and associated material handling processes. Empirical production data was used for parameterization and validation, while structured verification was performed throughout the model lifecycle.
  • Data-driven model calibration using historical production datasets
  • Stepwise verification of process logic and system interactions
  • Scenario design incorporating stochastic demand inputs from upstream processes
  • Throughput analysis and bottleneck detection under multiple crane configurations
The model supported quantitative evaluation of crane scenarios based on utilization, throughput, and queue dynamics. Task allocation for the new crane was structured to balance system load and minimize bottlenecks. The impact of pull-based upstream processes on system performance was also analyzed. An interactive dashboard was developed to visualize KPI behavior across scenarios.

Wind Industry · Germany · Heuristic Algorithm
Wind Turbine Installation Optimisation
AnyLogicJavaDoEMinitab
Wind turbine installation operations are highly sensitive to wind-speed-related constraints, which significantly impact installation duration and project execution efficiency. Planning methods often lack the ability to quantify how variations in component-level wind tolerance affect overall system performance under uncertain environmental conditions.
A DES of the full logistics chain in AnyLogic was paired with a custom Tabu Search algorithm in Java. It iteratively rescheduled tasks around weather forecasts to minimise constraint violations.
  • Stochastic weather modelling from historical European wind data
  • Greedy heuristic in Java for constrained schedule optimisation
  • Full factorial DoE to identify critical driver variables
  • ANOVA and interaction analysis validated in Minitab
The analysis showed that increasing component-level wind tolerance consistently reduces installation time across all evaluated scenarios. Sensitivity analysis identified the nacelle as the most critical component influencing overall installation performance. The results further demonstrated that operational strategies alone are not sufficient, as system performance remains strongly affected by wind variability and shift-dependent execution patterns.

3D Bin Packing Optimization System
PythonCombinatorial Optimization
This project addresses the 3D bin packing problem commonly found in manufacturing, logistics, and automated packaging systems. The objective is to efficiently arrange parts of varying dimensions inside available boxes while maximizing space utilization and ensuring no overlaps or boundary violations.
A hybrid optimization strategy was implemented, combining a greedy constructive heuristic with simulated annealing. The greedy phase builds an initial feasible solution using layered skyline-based placement, while the simulated annealing phase iteratively improves it through random swaps, rotations, and repositioning.
  • Greedy skyline-based initial placement
  • 3D collision + boundary checks
  • Simulated annealing for optimization (swap, rotate, move)
The system successfully generates valid packing configurations across multiple box sizes, ensuring all parts are placed without collision. It achieves improved space utilization by reducing unused volume through optimization and adapts effectively to different container dimensions.
